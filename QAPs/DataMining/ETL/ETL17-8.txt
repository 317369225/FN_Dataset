★Why should I use an existing ETL vs writing my own in Python for my data warehouse needs?I've been reading the Kimball Group Reader and they strongly recommend I use an existing ETL. Looking at solutions such as Kettle, SnapLogic, and Talend it seems their GUIs complicate the solution instead of making it easier to model and understand.
What platform are you targeting? If it's Oracle, use Oracle Warehouse Builder. For other platforms... well, use something-- don't hand-code. Once you get above a certain level of complexity (number of sources and targets, variety of transformations, and especially DW-related features like loading SCDs), you will find hand-coding harder and harder to maintain. No one will be able to look at your hand-code and see what you were trying to get done with it. Further, what metadata will you have about what you've built-- your sources and targets, what maps to where...    Embed Quote
