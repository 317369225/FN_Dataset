Time to render the page above-the-fold is what ultimately impacts user perception.  That's an aggregate of server latency, network latency, and page rendering time. You can report it with several numbers, the median, the top, and bottom 15%, something like that, or make a graph. Be consistent in how you report results, though, because its the nature of statistics that they can be carved up in a dozen different ways and come to multiple opposite conclusions, which causes confusion:  Simpson's paradox    Embed Quote
