★For an enterprise (say Coca-Cola or J. P. Morgan), is the unstructured data a big deal? Should a CIO really have a big data strategy?
Tech companies may be able to collate the various forms of unstructured data, but you would still need to personally manage the risk associated with the data and its use (data governance). Unstructured data may take many forms beyond social, such as video, sound, photographs, images, text and more. Whether or not the data is a big deal for the organization depends on the strategy of the organization, and the extent to which data is an integral part of the realization of that strategy. Pursuing data for data's sake is normally not the most productive way of leveraging the resource (or asset)    Embed Quote
