Are all NoSQL databases based on a distributed hash table? If yes, then how do they handle range queries?
No. Have a look at HBase and other NoSQL based on it. It uses range partitioning : http://hbase.apache.org/book/row... But, many of the other NoSQL databases do some form of hash partitioning. This generally makes sense for most data that is entity-centric -- data you would find in most databases. For example, the entities in EBay's databases would be users, listings, offers (on those listings). Entities in Netflix's databases would be users, movies, and ratings.
