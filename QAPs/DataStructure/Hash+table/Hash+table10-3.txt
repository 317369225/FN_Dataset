What are hash tables and why are they useful?
In slightly more technical terms, hashtables are an index (or map) of where data is located.  Joseph's example of a library is very good.  Imagine trying to locate a book in a library, by picking up the book nearest the front door, then the next book, then the next book.... it would take a very long time to find the book you want.  Instead, you look in the library index, that gives you an idea where the book is located to within a few feet.
