What makes acting believable?
Verisimilitude. That is, the quality of being truthful or real. Believability is the audience's perception of truthfulness in an actor's portrayal of the character, living in the imaginary world presented by the script. While the method or techniques an actor may employ to get to that level of truthfulness can vary, I believe there's a commonality in doing what we do to achieve a strong, believable performance: connecting to the work through our imagination and making it as personal to our circumstances as possible. Or to put it more eloquently, in the words of Sir Ian McKellan: "I imagined what it would be like to be a wizard - and then I acted..." Sir Ian McKellen, On Acting
