How does depth-first search (DFS) work?
Imagine it like this: You have a graph of cities, i.e. a map. You start from a city and then go to one of its neighbors (of course, there must be a road between these 2 cities). When you are in the neighbor city, you go to one of its neighboring cities. All this time you keep a checklist and mark each visited city, so you don't visit the same city twice. At some point you will reach a city from where you can go to no other city (because all its neighbors are visited).
