Mr. Carlyle lays out a wonderfully clear picture of where we are - but not a defense of why we need to continue.  Rather, the continued "to big to fail" mindset by significant sectors of the economy prevent serious search for alternative behaviors.      Consider this paragraph from the International Energy Agency 2012 report in the section discussing climate change: No more than one-third of proven reserves of fossil fuels can be consumed prior to 2050 if the world is to achieve the 2 °C goal, unless carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology is widely deployed.
