Would checking data structures like bloom filters, fibonacci heaps etc. (ones that are not very common) help you get a tech startup idea?
It's unlikely to lead very directly to a successful startup idea, because you wouldn't be searching in the most promising of places. Good startup ideas usually come from looking at what needs or problems people have, and then finding ways to help them address those needs or solve those problems. If you look for a startup idea by learning data structures, you're doing the whole process backwards. Instead of a chain of thinking that looks like What problem can I solve for people?
