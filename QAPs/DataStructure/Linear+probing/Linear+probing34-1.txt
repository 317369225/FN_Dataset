How can Voyager send a signal strong enough for us to receive, in spite of its enormous distance from us?
Radio. Voyager 1 is a great example (I actually heard this from a co-worker at JPL circa 2004). V1 is designed to communicate outside the boundaries of our solar system. On Earth, the radio waves are received and translated by the Deep Space Network. In the event of failure, the probe is equipped with a recorder and a mechanism to re-broadcast. You can read about the communication systems here: http://descanso.jpl.nasa.gov/DPS....
