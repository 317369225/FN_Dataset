Who are the most accomplished female scientists?
Carol Greider (Carol W. Greider) and Elizabeth Blackburn (Elizabeth Blackburn), who received a Nobel Prize for the discovery of telomerase (The 2009 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine; http://www.nytimes.com/2009/10/0...). Telomerase is the enzyme that builds the ends of the chromosomes; it plays a role in aging, stem cells, and cancer. They shared the Prize with Jack Szostak.
