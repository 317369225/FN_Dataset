Q. “If I have a database with 100 billion usernames, how do I efficiently built a sorted array from that to easily perform a binary search?” Unless you have a terabyte of RAM, you’d have to access your hard drive, a lot. Unless you have a big budget to buy a terabyte worth of SSD, you’d have to use a spinning hard drive. Assuming you have a spinning hard drive, random access will be very expensive — 10–100 ms expensive. So you’d like to minimize those as much as you can.
