What are the most ingenious theorems/deductions in graph theory?
Although still a conjecture. it appears fairly simple to understand and intuitively correct. Called the Ulams Conjecture.  Let graph [math]G[/math] have  [math]p[/math] points  [math]v_i[/math] and graph  [math]H[/math] hjave  [math]p[/math] points  [math]u_i[/math], where  [math]p\ge3[/math]. Then if for each  [math]i[/math], the subgraphs [math]G_i = G-v_i[/math] and [math]H_i = H-u_i[/math] are isomorphic, then the graphs [math]G[/math] and [math]H[/math] are isomorphic.
