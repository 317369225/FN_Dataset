Why is divisibility by two (even/odd) considered an important quality for numbers? Why not divisibility by 3? Or 17?
One very interesting property of even and odd numbers, which turns up in all sorts of mathematical proofs, is that two consecutive numbers are always one odd and one even. That is only true for divisibility by two, and not for divisibility by any other number. Edit to add: I hope you liked this answer.
