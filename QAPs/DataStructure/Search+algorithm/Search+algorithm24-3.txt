Can you explain how Google's search algorithm works in an easy to understand way?
Content, Site Architecture, and Inbound Links. In general, one can define search engine optimization as a collection of marketing and site development best practices that any self-respecting site owner should be doing anyway, even if search engines didn't exist. A website's reason for existing is to provide content to users; hopefully unique and interesting content about a specific subject.  This content should live on a properly built website.  If you get the first two correct, then other sites tend to willfully link to you as a resource.
