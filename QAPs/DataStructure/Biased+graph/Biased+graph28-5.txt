What is an assumption that people often wrongly make?
In one of the most inspiring TED talks ever, Dan Gilbert states that our pre-frontal cortex is a powerful experience simulator. We try to anticipate future events every day. The problem is that we're not always right on the evaluation of the consequences. As in the first example Dan presented: who do you think is happier between a lottery winner and a paraplegic, one year after winning vs. losing? The answer seems really simple.
