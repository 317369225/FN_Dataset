In which episode of Breaking Bad does Walter White become the bad guy? At what point or points does Walt make the jump from terminally-ill-guy-acting-out-of-necessity to something more sinister?
He doesn't ever become the bad guy, and he never will. Walter White is a man controlled by his surroundings, vainly attempting to assert his independence but stymied at every turn. Every time he (and you as the audience) think that he is going to catch a break, he just gets thrust into a new environment, that is more dangerous and with higher stakes than the last. That's what makes this show so compelling, and so relatable. He gets promoted to the big leagues, only to find himself a small fish once again.
