What do people think of the TensorFlow sucks article?
I think that TensorFlow's heavy graph orientation (as opposed to imperative define-by-run orientation) is both its greatest strength and greatest weakness. It's what makes the system amenable to many optimizations and makes it an excellent choice for production deployment (since there is no language runtime, just a graph). These things matter a great deal to Google and I believe ultimately to most industrial-grade customers so I think will be on-balance positive.
