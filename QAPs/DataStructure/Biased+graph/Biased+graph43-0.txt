As of 2017, is the Semantic Web field dead?
Um, it’s a matter of semantics. The term “semantic web” was associated with a grandiose vision beginning in 2001 with the publication of a Tim-Berners Lee article in Scientific American, back in the heyday of the public web. Tim Berners-Lee, who pioneered the World Wide Web, helped found the World Wide Web Consortium, a standards organization to advance the idea. His efforts and those of the W3C at large resulted in a stack of semantic web standards used in many companies today.
