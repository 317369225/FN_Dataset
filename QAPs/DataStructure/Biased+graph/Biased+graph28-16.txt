What is an assumption that people often wrongly make?
Some thoughts: That correlation equals causation - People consistently assume that when similar things occur, that one caused the other. Or that they are even related events. That there is a single cause for an event - Casual oversimplification. That single cause, instead of myriad events, led to a single outcome. That the middle ground is always the correct assumption - One extreme or the other may just as well be the correct answer. People assume that the "truth" always lies in the middle of extremes.
