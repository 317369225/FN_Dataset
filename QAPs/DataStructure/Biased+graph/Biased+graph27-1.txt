What is the intuitive explanation of power law distributions?
Here is how I think about it: A "normal" distribution arises when a set of many independent or random events or decisions are considered. A "power law" distribution arises when feedback from an initial or critical mass set of events or decisions influences future events or decisions. This is why almost all power law outcomes are an outgrowth of positive network effects of some form. This is true of popular movies, popular web sites, populous cities, and popular smart phone apps.
