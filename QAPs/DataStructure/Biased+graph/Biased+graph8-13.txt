How can intelligent people be gullible?
Intelligent people can be naive. They may think everyone is a person of good will. They are easily taken in by the con artist. One technique con artists use is to say “ I'm sure you know …” and then say something you don't know. Like “"I'm sure you know about the rule of 72.” Smart people are more reluctant to admit they don't know than persons closer to average intelligence. The best way to short circuit that con is to adopt Socratic ignorance: “No I don't. You seem to know a lot about the rule of 72. Can you explain it to me?” Keep asking questions until they are sorry they brought it up. ... (more)
