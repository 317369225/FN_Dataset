Considering the massive distance between the Earth and the Sun, how come a slight tilt is enough to change Earth's atmosphere during different seasons?
The questioner surely doesn’t live in the UK or further North! The tilt is not slight. It’s completely obvious if you live here, with eighteen-hour summer days with the sun high in the sky, and six-hour winter days with the sun just about far enough into the sky that it gets light. Three times as many hours of sunlight that’s also stronger for coming more directly downwards makes a huge difference! Experiment with a globe and a spotlight to see why.
