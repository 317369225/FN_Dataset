Are most gun homicides gang/criminal related, domestic, or other?
There are no authoritative national numbers for these sorts of things. But here are a couple of reputable statistics to help get things in focus. A study in Boston (Youth Violence in Boston: Gun Markets, Serious Youth Offenders, and a Use-Reduction Strategy) showed that 75% of homicide victims had prior criminal convictions. This doesn’t mean that they were all gang members, but certainly a substantial number would have been. Mostly what this shows is that being a criminal is dangerous work.
