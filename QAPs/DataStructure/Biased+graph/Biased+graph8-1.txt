How can intelligent people be gullible?
Many different ways. As Justin Franco answered, cognitive bias can lead to being gullible. General naiveté or innocence can result in gullibility, even with great intelligence. Highly intelligent children may still believe their parents when they say Santa Clause is real, because their parents told them, and all their friends “know” about him too, so why should they question it? Trusting personality. Some people have a tendency to trust others, and it doesn’t occur to them people will try to trick them. They don’t look for the lies, so they don’t see them. Narrow focus.
