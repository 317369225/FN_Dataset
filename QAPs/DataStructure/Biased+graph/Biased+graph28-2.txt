What is an assumption that people often wrongly make?
That correlation implies causation. Or similarly, that correlation can be used to prove causation. This common assumption is made across all layers of education and intelligence, and is the cause of many false conclusions in academic research. In a widely studied example, numerous epidemiological studies showed that women who were taking combined hormone replacement therapy (HRT) also had a lower-than-average incidence of coronary heart disease (CHD), leading doctors to propose that HRT was protective against CHD.
