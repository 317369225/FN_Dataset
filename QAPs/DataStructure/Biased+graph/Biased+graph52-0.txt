What are the different types of media bias?
According to McQuail (1993), there are four basic types of media bias. partisan (deliberate, admitted) - preference towards one party or the other, the newspaper is open about it and usually discloses this in an editorial or a comment propaganda (deliberate, hidden) - the newspaper tries to spin info for someone's agenda, without disclosing this deliberate inclination, this is a clear manipulation unwitting (admitted, unconscious) - some bias comes from the media routines, the technology and the way journalists work.
