History of Europe: Who won the Thirty Years' War?
France is the undisputed winner of the Thirty Years War, and in particular the policies of Cardinal de Richelieu. At the end of the war, Germany was put out of commission for two hundred years and the specter of German unification as well. France emerged as the dominant power in Europe, setting the stage for the century of Louis XIV. The realpolitik view of international relationship of Cardinal Richelieu also won the day, and the era of "la raison d'état" began.
