Why is the generative/discriminative distinction important in machine learning?
Thanks for the A2A Let us start with a simple example. We are given input samples [math]x[/math] and we want to classify its output label [math]y[/math]. A generative model learns a distribution [math]p(x,y)[/math] and discriminative model learns [math]p(y|x)[/math]. Consider the pairs [1] (1,0),(1,0),(2,0),(2,1) [math]p(x,y)[/math] can be explained as                              [math]y=0[/math]              [math]y=1[/math] [math]x=1[/math]  |           0.5                                   0 -----------------------   | [math]x=2[/math]  |            0.5                                0.25 Similarly [math]p(y|x)[/math] can be explained by the CPT                              [math]y=0[/math]              [math]y=1[/math] [math]x=1[/math]  |           1                                   0 -----------------------   | [math]x=2[/math]  |            0.5                                0.5 You can clearly see the difference between the two matrices (See the sum over rows) The 2nd matrix shows that [math]p(y|x)[/math] is a intutive way to discriminate between classes given an input. On the other hand generative algorithms are useful for generating likely ways of (x,y) pairs.
