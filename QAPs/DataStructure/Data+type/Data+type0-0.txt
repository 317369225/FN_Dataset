What are abstract data types?
An abstract data type (ADT) is basically a logical description or a specification of components of the data and the operations that are allowed, that is independent of the implementation. ADTs are a theoretical concept in computer science, used in the design and analysis of algorithms, data structures, and software systems, and do not correspond to specific features of computer languages. There may be thousands of ways in which a given ADT can be implemented, even when the coding language remains constant.
