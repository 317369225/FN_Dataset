Can you help me solve e x =1−x  ?
Building on Makarands answer: The real solution is the obvious one: x = 0 There are many complex solutions: e x =1−x  e a e ib =1−a−ib  With Euler: e a (cos(b)+isin(b))=1−a−ib  Taking real and imaginary parts: e a cosb=1−a  e a sinb=−b  cosb=1−ae a    sinb=−be a    Now we square these to get something we know (i.e. 1=sin 2 x+cos 2 x  ) cos 2 b=(1−a) 2 e 2a    sin 2 b=b 2 e 2a    Adding these gives: sin 2 b+cos 2 b=1=(1−a) 2 e 2a  +b 2 e 2a    And we have an answer that gives us intuition for the possible complex answers:  e 2a =(1−a) 2 +b 2   So for any positive real part of a complex number there are imaginary parts b=(e 2a −(1−a) 2 ) − − − − − − − − − − − −  √   that works for this equation!
