What does AOL get with the about.me acquisition?
For AOL, About.me (with a pinch of social) could become the foundation for their own social network. Make it easy for folks to create content about themselves, allow them to “follow” or “connect” with others, and slowly you’re building a social network that can compete with the rest. Add a search box tomorrow and you’re on your way to a better LinkedIn. Not bad. About.me also has a few other things going for it: vanity, online identity aggregation, personal analytics, ease of use (less work than even tumblr/ posterous). Besides it doesn’t hurt that the domain name is pretty hot and the team is a bunch of rockstars. Now let’s see what AOL does with it.
