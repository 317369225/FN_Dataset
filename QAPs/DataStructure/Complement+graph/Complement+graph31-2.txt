Linguistic Relativity: If language influences thinking, what is the "best" language to complement English?
As far as cognition-shifting bang for the language-learning buck goes, consider E-prime: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E-P... Basically, it's English minus the verb "to be". That seems like a small change, but it drags you out into deep philosophical waters. You quickly discover how much English helps opinion masquerade as fact. E.g., consider the difference between these statements: Quora is useless. I couldn't find a way to use Quora effectively. When people say things of the first sort, they often mean something like the second. But the literal meaning of those sentences and the way they shape following conversations is very different.
