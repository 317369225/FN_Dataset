What are some brain hacks that a neuroscientist or a psychologist knows that most people don't?
Mind-Body Dissonance can make one think expansively. "In a clever series of studies, they developed a way to get people’s facial expressions to depart from their emotional experiences.  Participants were asked to either hold a pen between their teeth, forcing an unwitting smile, or to affix two golf tees in a particular position on their foreheads, unwittingly forcing an expression of sadness.  While in these facial configurations subjects were asked to recall happy and sad events or listen to happy and sad music." A New Tool for Creative Thinking: “Mind-Body Dissonance”
