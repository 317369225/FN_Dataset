What are the 17 greatest mathematical 'break throughs' ever?

German) 2 Method of Exhaustion / Summation of Infinite Series (Archimedes, Greek) 3 Calculus / Newton's Method (Sir Isaac Newton, English) 4 Euclidean Geometry (Euclid, Greek) 5 Analytical Geometry / Co-ordinate System (Rene Descartes, French) 6 Pythagoras Theorem (Pythagoras, Greek) 7 Set Theory (Georg Cantor, Russian) 8 Method of Least Squares (Adrien-Marie Legendre, French) 9 Fermat's Last Theorem / Fermat's Little Theorem (Pierre de Fermat, French) 10 Logarithm Tables (John Napier, Scottish) 11 Boolean Logic (George Boole, English) 12 Law of Sines / Spherical trigonometry (Nasir al-Din al-Tusi, Persian) 13 Quaternion Group (William Hamilton, Irish) 14 Complex Analysis / Functions / Topology / Euler's method / Graph Theory (Leonhard Euler, Swiss) 15 Fourier Series / Fourier Transform (Joseph Fourier, French) 16 Binary number system / Principle of Reasoning / Matrix / Mathematical Logic (Gottfried Leibniz, German) 17 De Morgan's Law / Universal Algebra (Augustus De Morgan, British) Moivre, French) Group Theory /  Lagrange interpolation polynomial (Joseph Louis Lagrange, French) Non-Euclidean Geometry (Alhazen, Arab) Work on Probability / Pascal's Triangle (Blaise Pascal, French)
