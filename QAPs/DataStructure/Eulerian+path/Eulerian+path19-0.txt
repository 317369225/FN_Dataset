What are the must-reads for computer scientists new to the bioinformatics field?
I made a list of papers for a mathematician, statistician or a computer scientist new to bioinformatics. It can be found here - Diving into bioinformatics : Introductory studies for a mathematician/statistician or a computer scientist by Janu Verma on Ripples in Water I'm reproducing the list here. 1.  Sequencing NGS : Jay Shendure, Hanlee Ji (2008) Next-generation DNA sequencingNature Biotechnology 26, 1135-1145(2008) 2.  Alignment: BLAST (Basic Local Alignment Search Tool) BowTie: Ben Langmead, Cole Trapnell, Mihai Pop, Steven Salzberg. (2009)Ultrafast and memory-efficient alignment of short DNA sequences to the human genome Genome Biology 2009, 10:R25 BWA: Li H. and Durbin R. (2009) Fast and accurate short read alignment with Burrows-Wheeler transform. Bioinformatics, 25, 1754-1760. BWA-MEM: Li H. (2013) Aligning sequence reads, clone sequences and assembly contigs with BWA-MEM.  3.  Assembly: de Bruijn graphs: Phillip E C Compeau, Pavel A Pavzner, Glenn Tesler (2011). How to apply de Bruijn graphs to genome assembly  Nature Biotechnology 29, 987-991(2011). de Bruijn Assembly: Pavel Pevzner, Haixu Tang, Michael Waterman (2001) An Eulerian path approach to DNA fragment assembly PNAS 98, 9748-9753 Velvet: D.R. Zerbino and E. Birner (2008) Velvet: algorithms for de novo short read assembly using de Bruijn graphs.  Genome Research 18:821-829 Evaluation: Keith Bradnam et al (2013) Assemblathon 2: evaluating de novo methods of genome assembly in three vertebrate species. GigaScience 2:10 (2013) 4.  SNP calling: GATK 1: McKenna A et al (2010) The Genome Analysis Toolkit : A MapReduce framework for analyzing next-generation DNA sequencing data. Genome Research 20:1297-1303 GATK 2: DePristo M et al (2011) A framework for variation and genotyping using next-generation DNA sequencing data. Nature Genetics 43:491-498 FreeBayes: Haplotype-based variant detection from short-read sequencing 5.  Hidden Markov Models: Byubg-Jun Yoon (2008) Hidden Markov models and their applications in biological sequence analysis 6.  GWAS: David J. Balding (2006) A tutorial on statistical methods for population association studies. Nature Reviews Genetics 7, 781-791. Witte J.S. (2010) Genome-wide association studies and beyond. Annual Rev Public Health. 2010;31:9-20 Thomas A. Peason How to Interpret a Genome-wide Association Study. JAMA. 2008;299(11):1335-1344 7.  Principle Component Analysis in GWAS: Alkes Price et al (2006) Principal components analysis corrects for stratification in genome-wide association studies Nature Genetics 38, 904-909 Appendix : File formats: FASTA format FASTQ format GFF - (GFF3 - GMOD), (The Sequence Ontology - Resources - GFF3) VCF SAM/BAM
