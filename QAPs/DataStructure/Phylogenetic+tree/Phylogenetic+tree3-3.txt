How do you interpret ancestry in phylogenetic trees?
The problem is: that’s a cladogram, not a phylogenetic tree. The difference is that cladograms don’t identify common ancestors. They only estimate genetic distances. So you can tell from the diagram that (of the genera listed) modern elephants are most closely related to mammoths, but you can’t tell what was the common ancestor of modern elephants and mammoths. Based only on the diagram, it could have been a modern elephant, or a mammoth, or some third unmentioned genus. The same applies to moeritheria.
