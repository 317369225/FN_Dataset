What is a good phylogenetic tree of human population groups?
Be advised that human population groups do not form a tree, because populations rejoin. For example, when Marco Polo traveled from Italy to China, he made contact between two populations that had not mixed, in the sense of having children together, for a long time. I don't know whether his European party left half-European children behind in China, but I wouldn't be surprised if they did. It is more certain that in modern times, British colonies around the world have generated children between British and local parents.
