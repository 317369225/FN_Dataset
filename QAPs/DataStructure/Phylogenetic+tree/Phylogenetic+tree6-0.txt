Is it possible to build a phylogenetic tree of Abrahamic religions according to their similarities and differences?
A religion is different than a story. Every instance of a story is a complete text which may be compared to other instances of the story and to other stories. But what is an instance of a religion? Each religion includes a wide variety of beliefs and customs, and probably no two believers share the same set of beliefs. And the variations overlap. Some Muslim are more tolerant towards nob-believers than some Christians, but the opposite is also true. Where would you place the level of tolerance of "Christianity" and of "Islam"?
