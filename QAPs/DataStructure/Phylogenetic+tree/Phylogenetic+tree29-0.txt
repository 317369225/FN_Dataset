What would a phylogenetic tree of the races of Middle Earth look like?
Middle-Earth uses what we might call “fairy-tale biology.” Anything reasonably humanoid can mate with anything else reasonably humanoid, producing elf-human crosses, and orc-human crosses. (Carl Sagan once pointed out that Mr. Spock of Star Trek, a Vulcan-human cross, was about as likely as the successful mating of a man and a petunia.) But as Gwydion Madawc Williams has pointed out, Tolkien postulated separate creation for Elves, Dwarves and Men, and the Vala Yavanna personally made all the plants and animals individually.
