Let [math]R[/math] be the relation defined on the set of ordered pairs [math]\mathbb{Z}^{+}\times \mathbb{Z}^{+}[/math]of positive integers defined by [math](a,b)R(c,d)\Leftrightarrow a+d = b+c[/math] . (1)  prove that [math]R[/math] is an equivalence relation on  [math]\mathbb{Z}^{+}\times \mathbb{Z}^{+}[/math]. (2)  list 5 different members of the equivalence class [math][(1,4)][/math] ?
Let [math](x,y) \in \mathbb{Z}^{+} \times \mathbb{Z}^{+}[/math]. [math]x+y=y+x[/math], so [math](x,y)R(x,y)[/math]. Let [math](x,y), (w,z) \in \mathbb{Z}^{+} \times \mathbb{Z}^{+}[/math]. First, [math]x+z=y+w[/math] since [math](x,y)R(w,z)[/math]. So, [math]w+y=z+x[/math]. Hence, [math](w,z)R(x,y)[/math].   Let [math](x,y), (w,z), (u,v) \in \mathbb{Z}^{+} \times \mathbb{Z}^{+}[/math]. If [math](x,y)R(w,z)[/math] and [math](w,z)R(u,v)[/math], is [math](x,y)R(u,v)[/math]? This is for you to do.
