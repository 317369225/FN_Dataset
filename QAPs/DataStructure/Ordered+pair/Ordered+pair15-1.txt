What is the number of ordered pairs (n,m) of positive integers that satisfy n^3+7n−133=m^3?
Let m = n then it gives n as 19 and m = 19 Let m = n + k, then         [math]3kn^2 + (3k^2-7)n + k^3 + 133 = 0[/math] this give         [math]n = \frac{-3k^2 + 7 \pm \sqrt{49-1596k-42k^2-3k^4}}{6k}[/math] In the above k can not be greater than 0 and less than -7 as the expression in the square root will be negative. So possible values of k are -1, -2, -3, -4, -5, -6, and -7.        k       n                 m       -1       6                  5       -2       5                 3       -3     irrational       -4     irrational       -5     irrational       -6       1                 -5 So the only positive integers solutions are (6, 5), (5, 3), and (19, 19).
