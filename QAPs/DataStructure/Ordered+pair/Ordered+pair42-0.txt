Two 1×1 squares are chosen from an 8×8 chessboard. What is the probability that they belong to the same 4×4 square?
There are [math]8^2[/math] ways to pick [math]1 \le i, j \le 8[/math]. Of these, the number such that [math]j - i \ge 4[/math] is, by substituting [math]j' = j - 3[/math], the same as the number of ways to pick [math]1 \le i < j' \le 5[/math], which is [math]\tbinom{5}{2}[/math]. The number such that [math]i - j \ge 4[/math] is also [math]\tbinom{5}{2}[/math]. So, of the [math](8^2)^2 - 8^2[/math] ordered pairs of different squares [math](x_1, y_1)[/math] and [math](x_2, y_2)[/math], only [math]\bigl(8^2 - 2\tbinom{5}{2}\bigr)^2 - 8^2[/math] satisfy [math]|x_1 - x_2| < 4[/math] and [math]|y_1 - y_2| < 4[/math], whence the desired probability is   [math]\frac{\bigl(8^2 - 2\tbinom{5}{2}\bigr)^2 - 8^2}{(8^2)^2 - 8^2} = \frac{13}{28}[/math]. (This assumes squares are drawn without replacement. Other reasonable assumptions yield answers of [math]\tfrac{121}{256}[/math] or [math]\tfrac{25}{52}[/math].) Updated 130w ago • View Upvotes
