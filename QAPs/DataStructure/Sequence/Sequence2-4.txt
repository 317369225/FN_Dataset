What will be next in the sequence 10, 14, 23, ____?
Actually ,it is a meaningless question since any number will give the correct answer. ie It is possible to find a function [math]f(n) [/math] such that [math]f(1)=10, f(2)=14, f(3)=23[/math] and [math]f(4)=k ,[/math]  which can be any pre- assigned number ( ie any number of our choice!) Finding the function f(n) is easy : Take [math]f(n)= a.(n-2)(n-3)(n-4) + b.(n-1)(n-3)(n-4) [/math]                                                   [math]+ c.(n-1)(n-2)(n-4)..............[/math][math](A).[/math] We can easily find the constants [math]a, b, c[/math]  by putting [math]n=1, n=2[/math]  and [math]n=3[/math]  in equation [math](A).[/math] But we can ask to find a [math]simple[/math]    relation among the terms to predict the next term. The following tips will help you to find some relations: Suppose, the first  three terms of a sequence  [math]a_{1} , a_{2} , a_{3}[/math]  are  given. 1. First check whether the given sequence is in A.P or  G.P ( ie check there is     any    common difference or common ratio) If it is so, we can easily predict the      next term. 2. Take the differences: [math]a_{2}- [/math][math]a_{1} , a_{3} - a_{2}[/math]    and check whether      they are in A.P or G.P.  If so we can easily find  the next term [math] a_{4} - a_{3} [/math]      by  adding the three  terms [math]a_{2}- [/math][math]a_{1}[/math] , [math]a_{3} - a_{2}[/math] ,    [math]a_{4} - a_{3}[/math]    we can easily calculate [math] [/math][math] a_{4} .[/math] 3. Compare with other familiar sequences like sequence of primes, sequence of squares of natural numbers, squares of primes, cubes of primes ,  etc In this case  , the differences are  4 ,  9   which are perfect squares of natural numbers from 2.   The next difference is 16 and hence the required fourth term of the original sequence is   [math]39.[/math]
