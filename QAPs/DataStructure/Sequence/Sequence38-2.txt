What is the next term of sequence [math]\frac{\pi}{2},\;\frac{\pi}{2},\;\frac{\pi}{2},\;\frac{\pi}{2},\;\frac{\pi}{2},\;\frac{\pi}{2},\;\frac{\pi}{2}[/math]? I say it's [math]\frac{467807924713440738696537864469}{935615849440640907310521750000}\pi[/math]. Why? The sequence above is formed from the Borwein integrals [math]\int_0^{\infty}\prod_{k=1}^n\frac{\sin\frac{x}{2k-1}}{\frac{x}{2k-1}}dx[/math] for [math]n=1,2,\ldots,7[/math]. For [math]n=8[/math], you don't get [math]\frac{\pi}{2}[/math], though. You get the number above, or approximately [math]0.499999999992646\pi[/math].
