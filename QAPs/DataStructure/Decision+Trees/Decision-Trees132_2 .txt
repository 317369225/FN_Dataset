What is an intuitive explanation of a decision tree?I would say, that at least from a business decision point of view, a decision tree is the minimum number of yes/no questions that one has to ask, to assess the probability of making a correct decision, most of the time A classic example is diagnosing a heart attack. The only sure way of doing this is by admitting a patient to the hospital and subjecting the patient to testing. This is time consuming and expensive. Interestingly, cardiologist Lee Goldman (who worked aboard a submarine) spend years developing a decision tree which did this efficiently. This helped the staff determine whether or not the sub needed to resurface in order to help the chest pain sufferer!
