What are some interesting facts about chemistry?
The chemist Glenn Seaborg was the only person in the world who could write his address in chemical elements: Sg, Lr, Bk, Cf, Am.Â  Each of these are transuranium elements discovered at the laboratory where Seaborg worked, and were conveniently named: 1. Seaborgium (Sg), named after Seaborg himself. 2. Lawrencium (Lr), named after the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. 3. Berkelium (Bk), named after the city of Berkeley, the home of UC Berkeley. 4. Californium (Cf), named after the state of California. 5. Americium (Am), named after America.
