What are the important theorems or properties of the eigenvalues of the adjacency matrix for a graph?
Let [math]G[/math] be an undirected simple graph of [math]n[/math] vertices with adjacency matrix [math]A \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}[/math]. If [math]G[/math] has diameter [math]d[/math], then [math]A[/math] has at least [math]d + 1[/math] distinct eigenvalues. Say the graph is [math]k[/math]-regular. Then [math]G[/math] is bipartite if and only if [math]-k[/math] is an eigenvalue of [math]A[/math]. Let [math]t[/math] be the number of triangles in [math]G[/math]. Then [math]tr~A^3 = 6t[/math]. If [math]G[/math] is a line graph, then its minimum eigenvalue is greater than or equal to [math]-2[/math]. If [math]G[/math] is [math]k[/math]-regular with least eigenvalue [math]\lambda[/math], then [math]\alpha(G) \leq -n\lambda/(k - \lambda)[/math], where [math]\alpha(G)[/math] is the size of the largest independent set in [math]G[/math].
