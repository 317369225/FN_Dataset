10BASE5, the original Ethernet cabling, which was the type primarily used in 1984, was cumbersome, expensive and not something that a typical Mac user would have access to. Thicknet, as it was called, would soon give way to 10BASE2, or Thinnet, which was more similar to LocalTalk's physical dimensions and had inexpensive termination requirements.
