There are traffic jams on Mount Everest Everest climbing is a big business currently. The narrow climbing window (May-September), and the practically unlimited distribution of permits by Nepal has created bottleneck problems. And according to mountaineers, it has made Everest more dangerous. As you stand, you lose heat, and the chances of hypothermia shoot up. Do read this National Geographic article (where this image is from) or this BBC one to get a sense of the human greed and tragedy that accompanies this picture. Update: After years of criticism that Nepal has done little to manage the growing number of Everest climbers despite making millions of dollars in fees, they may be taking steps to ease the Everest traffic jam.
