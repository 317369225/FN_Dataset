As Adrian Aoun points out, trademarkability is something to consider. In the U.S., you are more likely to get a trademark on what is called a "fanciful" name than a regular English-word name. This is why you see trademarks with words like "Kleen" substituted for "Clean" and "Kwik" substituted for "Quick. RE: Wavil, I met a guy who worked for a company called Truvel. He told me, "We put the 'U' in 'Travel'."
