★Are high blood pressure and diabetes always interconnected?
Diabetes and hypertension are partners in crime !! In a same individual diabetes and hypertension is very common , more than that can be explained by chance alone.  According to UKPDS study, people with hypertension have 2.5 times higher risk to develop diabetes than those having normal blood pressure . If a patient with diabetes lives long enough , there are more than 75% chances of him developing high blood pressure . So you see, diabetes and hypertension go arm in arm .   Both share similar predisposing factors : Such a close association may be be due to underlying common genetic and environmental factors . Obesity, lack of physical activity , insulin resistance , activated renin angiotensin system etc are common predisposing factors for both .    Embed Quote
