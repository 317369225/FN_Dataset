★Why are our memories and music so interconnected?
The most prominent theory is that the human brain evolved in the medial pre-frontal cortex when we started to communicate with each other through sound. This type of communication served as the prominent communication technique long before formal language or written language was ever created. Much of our brain capacity is dedicated to spatial memory (Read Moonwalking with Einstein: The Art and Science of Remembering Everything) Sequences of tones trigger memory units that are generally tied to images, so a person can remember places or people, friends or foes, things OK to eat. Music-Memory Connection Found in Brain This became increasingly important for pack animals to communicate things that were learned and pass that on to the rest of the pack or their children. The best way to associate those mental images were associations to a sequence of tones, which eventually turned into language.    Embed Quote
