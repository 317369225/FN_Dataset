In lay man's terms, it is just a container carrying data. In the concept of computer networks, there are many layers present. You can google for OSI model and TCP/IP model. Technically, the data represented at each layer of the models mentioned above is referred with specific terms. And, at the network layer it's called a packet. But, generally people refer to data moving in a network as packets, irrespective of the layer. You can imagine a packet to be like a post. The main contents of a post is a letter containing information to be conveyed. It is put inside a cover on which the mailing address is written. Similarly, packets contain data to be transferred from sender to receiver and the address of the both is also present in a packet. Along with these contents, additional details related to protocols to be followed, error codes, conditional flags may be present.
