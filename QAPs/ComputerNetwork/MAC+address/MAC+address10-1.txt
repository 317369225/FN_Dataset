★What can I learn about a device from its MAC address alone?
You can learn, for instance, that three firsts bytes are defined by the IEEE  Organizationally Unique Identifier (OUI) and the three last bytes are defined by the vendor. But the most important thing is know that this address is necessary to communication hop-to-hop. That is, intra-domain, node a node. No matter if the standard is  802.3, 802.11, 802.15, etc... You can also know when the address is unicast (00:02:72:9b:b0:19), broadcast (ff:ff:ff:ff:ff:ff) or multicast (47:20:1B:2E:08:EE). If the bit most significant of 1st octet is 0 the address is Unicast, If the bit most significant of 1st octet is 1 the address is Multicast. For instance: 47:20:1B:2E:08:EE --> 1st octet 47, binary notation 01000111 Multicast. 00:02:72:9b:b0:19   --> 1st octet 00, binary notation 00000000 Unicast. If all octets are 1 it's broadcast.
