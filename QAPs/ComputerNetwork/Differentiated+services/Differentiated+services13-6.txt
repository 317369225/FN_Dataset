While each of these companies has its characteristics that helped them grow, survive and thrive, we should not discount the possibility of Survivorship bias here (see the Wikipedia article for more). In general there are too many factors that can affect business decisions like selecting an outsourcing partner, negotiating deal terms, hiring the "right" people, etc.  Sometimes it's sheer luck / randomness that favours one company over another. Beyond a certain point, the scale of the company kicks in -- it's more visible in the market, the brand may get stronger, network effects help with hiring, operational efficiencies improve, etc. But in general, it's very hard to derive a formula for success out of the accumulated years of serendipity...
