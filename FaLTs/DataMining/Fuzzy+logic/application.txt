The Japanese were the first to utilize fuzzy logic for practical applications. The first notable application was on the high-speed train in Sendai, in which fuzzy logic was able to improve the economy, comfort, and precision of the ride.[6] It has also been used in recognition of hand written symbols in Sony pocket computers, Canon auto-focus technology, Omron auto-aiming cameras, earthquake prediction and modeling at the Institude of Seismology Bureau of Metrology in Japan, etc.
